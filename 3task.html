Maya and Leo were inseparable from the age of five.

They lived next door to each other, but they couldn’t be more different.

Maya was the "Anchor"—grounded, practical, and a quiet thinker.

Leo was the "Kite"—dreamy, spontaneous, and always chasing ideas.

Their friendship grew around a massive willow tree in the backyard.

It was their secret world.

When they were ten, Leo decided they were going to build something.

He didn't have a plan, just enthusiasm.

Maya spent three days studying a book on knots and wood.

When they finally launched it, Leo jumped on immediately.

Maya stepped on slowly, watching the structural integrity.

The raft held.

"You're the only one who lets me dream, Maya," Leo said, looking at the water.

"And you're the only one who makes me move, Leo," she replied with a smile.

Life, however, rarely allows for uninterrupted childhood.

When they were seventeen, Leo's father was transferred across the country.
